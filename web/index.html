What is an Urban Heat Island?

Urban heat islands are a phenomenon where surface and air temperature is hotter than their rural surroundings.

Why does it happen?

  • Urban construction materials are impermeable. Usually, water absorbs thermic energy and evaporets, cooling surfaces. If surfaces do not allow water to be around, heat increases
  • Most manmade materials retain more heat than natural materials
  • Constructions slow down or stop aiflow, preventing surfaces to cool1.
  • Heat produced by human activities: electrical devices, car engines, industries.
  • Lack of vegetation produces shade, retains or produces water, and releases water through evaporation.

What does it cause?

  • ENERGETIC DEMAND: Electronic and mechanic devices use more energy to cool down. This generates more greenhouse gasses and smog. Furthermore, when some surfaces heat, they release volatile greenhouse gasses emissions. Some industries use water to cool down machineries, also impacting on the water reservoir.
  • INVERSION CAP: During day, air heats and rises until certain limit. Hot air that is later generated cannot keep rising. The result is an inversion cap that encloses heat and smog.
  • HEALTH: The inversion cap affects health, causing higher cardiac output, low immune system abilities and pulmonary diseases, like lung irritation asthma aggravation, emphysema and bronchitis.
  • Heat waves can produce heat-illness, cardiovascular, cerebrovascular and respiratory traumas
  • CLIMATIC ALTERATIONS: Changes in the low pressure conditions of the inversion cap can produce alterations, like tornadoes.
    These systems produce more clouds, and therefore, more rain.
  • BIOTIC ALTERATIONS: All living beings have a temperature physiological range in which they survive. For example, in water, heat produces the loss of O2, needed by animals and plants7. In land, insects (that can be useful as plague control, polinization, food for birds, etc) are sensible to dehydration, and can die8. Changes in temperature can confound migration in birds9. In general, temperature affects metabolism and growth rates of species.
